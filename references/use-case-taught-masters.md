# Taught master's — what changes

This is the closest case to the base move framework, so the deltas are small.
A taught (coursework-based) MSc/MA is usually about gaining professional or
disciplinary capability, not about proving research potential. Calibrate
accordingly.

## What to emphasize

- **The gap and the gains carry the most weight.** The Reasons-for-Applying move
  — specifically the *Gap in Background → Positive Gains* sequence — is the
  engine of a taught-master's SOP. Make the gap concrete (what the applicant
  cannot do yet) and tie the gains to specific courses and the career outcome.
- **Professional ethos over research ethos.** Credibility comes from work
  experience, applied projects, and demonstrated capability, more than from
  publications or research methods. Lead with what the applicant has *done*.
- **Program fit = curriculum and outcomes.** Name specific modules, electives,
  the practical/dissertation component, industry links, or employability
  features. López-Ferrero & Bach (2016) found master's-level motivation letters
  characteristically connect *academic* and *professional* worlds — a strong
  taught-master's SOP bridges both: study now, in service of a work goal.
- **One clear career direction** in the conclusion, mapped through the program.

## What to de-emphasize

- A prior research history is welcome but **not required**. Do not manufacture a
  research narrative the applicant does not have; it reads as strain.
- Avoid over-theorizing. Taught programs want capable practitioners, not
  miniature PhD proposals.

## Disciplinary note

Samraj & Monk (2008) found that statements across disciplines share the same
moves but realize them through different *steps*. A taught-master's SOP in a
quantitative/technical field leans harder on demonstrated technical competence
(logos-heavy, specific tools and results); one in a professional or humanities
field leans more on motivation, fit, and articulated purpose. Read the
applicant's target field and weight the steps to match what that field values.

## Typical shape

- Length: honor the stated limit; ~650–750 words is a sound default where none is given.
- A research proposal is **not** usually expected. If the program asks for one
  separately, keep the SOP focused on purpose and fit and let the proposal carry
  the research detail.

## Sources

- Samraj & Monk (2008), English for Specific Purposes 27(2), 193–211.
- López-Ferrero & Bach (2016), Discourse Studies 18(3), 286–310 (note:
