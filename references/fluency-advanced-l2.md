# Advanced L2 — what changes

The applicant communicates well in English, with occasional non-native markers.
Provide light language support *and* genre scaffolding, while protecting their
authentic voice.

## Language: light-touch naturalness editing

Smooth the recurring L2 markers without sterilizing the writing:

- **Articles and prepositions** (a/the; "depend on", "interested in") — the most
  common residual slips; fix silently.
- **Collocations and word choice** — replace near-miss combinations calqued from
  the first language with the natural English collocation (e.g., "make a
  research" → "do research"; "since 5 years" → "for five years").
- **Register calibration** — L2 writers sometimes swing between overly formal
  ("Henceforth I aspire to...") and overly casual. Pull toward the formal-but-
  personal register the genre wants (see `references/style-and-voice.md`).
- **Do not over-correct into a generic "native" voice.** A trace of the writer's
  own cadence is authenticity, not error. Fix what impedes clarity or reads as a
  mistake; leave what is simply *theirs*.

## Genre: scaffold the moves, not just the sentences

A consistent EAP finding is that L2 (and novice L1) writers often have good
*declarative* knowledge of what a statement should contain but struggle to
*apply* it — the procedural and conditional knowledge of when and how to deploy
each move (Chiu's work on doctoral L2 applicants; broader genre-awareness
research). So the higher-value help here is structural:

- Give an explicit move-by-move outline before drafting.
- Check that the Gap → Gains sequence and the credentials/reasons interweaving
  actually happen — these are where applied genre knowledge tends to break down,
  regardless of sentence quality.

## Self-promotion calibration

Some L2 writers come from rhetorical cultures where overt self-promotion reads as
immodest, and under-claim as a result. The SOP genre *requires* explicit
competence claims. Coach the applicant to state accomplishments directly and
back them with evidence — confidence grounded in fact is the expected register,
not arrogance.
