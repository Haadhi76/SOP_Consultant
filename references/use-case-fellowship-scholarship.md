# Fellowship / scholarship / grant — what changes

A funded opportunity is judged against **published selection criteria** by a
funder with an explicit mission. That single fact drives every delta below. This
can be a standalone case or a modifier layered on a degree application.

## Answer the criteria, literally

Funders almost always publish what they are selecting for. Map the statement
directly onto those criteria — if the funder names academic merit, leadership,
and alignment with its mission, the SOP should visibly address each. Do not make
the committee hunt for the fit; structure the statement so the criteria are easy
to tick off while still reading as a coherent narrative.

## What gets weighted differently

- **Mission alignment / impact is genuinely scored.** Unlike ordinary
  admissions, where "give back to society" can read as filler, fellowships
  frequently select *for* it. Where the applicant's goals authentically serve
  the funder's purpose, make that connection explicit and specific. Where they
  do not, do not fake it — choose a different framing or a different funder.
- **Goals stated with conviction.** Fellowship guidance consistently calls for
  strong declarative language about goals ("I will", "I plan to") and warns
  against hedging across several possible futures or undercutting language ("if
  possible, I might like to"). Commit to one clear plan.
- **Feasibility, for research/project fellowships.** If the award funds a
  specific project or period of study, the statement should sketch a project
  that is concrete and *achievable* within the fellowship's scope and timeline.

## Intertextuality

A fellowship application is a *system* of documents — statement, CV, references,
sometimes a research plan. The statement should align with and reinforce the
others rather than duplicate them: it carries the motivation, the narrative
through-line, and the fit-with-mission that the CV and references cannot express.

## Typical shape

- Often shorter and more tightly prompted than a degree SOP. Respect the word
  limit strictly — funders read at volume.
- A workable spine: a competence claim grounded in evidence → motivation for
  *this* opportunity and field → fit with the funder's mission and the specific
  project/plan → a committed forward statement.
